Most university students have very busy schedules and often find themselves heating up frozen boxed food to eat just because it’s fast and easy and they don’t have to leave their apartment. But what if there was a way to eat the hot fresh made dining hall food without taking too much time away from busy schedules? The Grubhub app includes a feature that allows students to connect and pay with their campus cards and order food ahead from their campus dining halls to pick up their food on the way back to their dorms or apartment.

Project Overview

Designers need to design for error. This means both to reduce the opportunities to make errors and to accept that people will make errors and provide ways to mitigate their impact and recover from them. 

I, along with my three other teammates from our DSGN1 class selected the Grubhub app as our product to observe for our project and redesigned the campus card connection experience and layout of dining options and payment options to mitigate errors

Getting Started

My teammates and I interviewed UCSD students by asking general open-ended questions about their usage of the Grubhub app. We also had them go through tasks and observed how they interacted with the app. We were able to see step-by-step the process of ordering a drink from the app and make comparisons between users.

User Interview Main Insights

competitive analysis

We wanted to capture a better understanding of how other food ordering apps presented information and content and how that compares with Grubhub. We evaluated the navigability between the different apps to determine what we can incorporate into our redesign. 

our redesign

Easier campus card connection

To minimize error, we decided to add a campus logo at the top right corner of the landing page so users will not have to look for it so intensely. The logo ​affords​ clicking and it being a school logo ​signifies​ that it has to do with a college or campus. The school logo helps bridge the gulf of evaluation​ utilizing a ​system image​ that tells the user “hey click me! You can link your campus card here!”

Card UI + horizontal scrolling

To reduce the error of scrolling past a location, our redesign focuses on a horizontal scroll instead of a vertical scroll. Now the logo and name of the dining hall is big and centered making it hard for users to make an error in scrolling past what they want. The shadow behind each “box” ​signifies each individual dining hall. Being able to slightly see the next “box” also ​affords​ that you can scroll horizontally to view the next dining hall. 

Clearer payment selection

To tackle the issue where users would accidentally pay with the wrong payment method, in our redesign we decided to add an extra step to ask the user for their payment method. After selecting the payment method, there is one last chance to review the entire order to make sure everything is actually correct. After clicking “place your order” an extra frame is added to help bridge the ​gulf of evaluation​. Getting the green “order placed!” page is ​feedback to the users that their order has been successfully placed!

Reflection

This case study was built on a project from one of the courses I took and it was a pretty short project with a very specific goal my group and I were going towards. We specifically looked for errors and used design thinking to help reduce those errors. Campus dining might have changed since the time this project was done, and if so I 'd love to take on this challenge once again and iterate to make the campus dining ordering even better!
