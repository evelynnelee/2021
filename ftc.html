Imagine yourself as a middle school or high school student looking for an extracurricular activity to join. You enjoy being active and running but don’t know exactly what team you want to be involved with. Falcon Track Club trains athletes ages 7-18 in a competitive sense while making it a fun experience.

The problem

With outdated visuals and unorganized navigation, the current website makes it difficult to convey who Falcon Track Club is and intuitively communicate valuable information to its users.

the solution

Redesign the website to give a fresh modern look and to provide adequate information for prospective customers, but also for current athletes at FTC. 

research insights

Understanding our users

To understand the users needs and frustrations, my team and I conducted interviews with three different groups of users: current athletes , current athlete parents , and prospective customers .

Top use cases for the website based on each type of audience

We took a look at other track teams across Northern and Southern California and analyzed the aesthetics of their websites as well as who they were trying to reach with their websites.

We analyzed and found common trends among each competitor and used those trends to come up with key takeaways to help with our redesign.

Early stage designs

User testing insights

We wanted to get initial feedback from our lofi prototypes before we made higher fidelity prototypes. We asked interviewees to do several tasks and observed them while they talked through their process.

Some of our findings of usability errors include:

  • Visibility: Not being able to find the newsletter straight away
  • Difference in the designer’s and user’s mental model:  Unaware of the relationship between price of their services and going to the Join FTC page
  • Difference in the designer’s and user’s mental model: Misjudged where to find information on if throwing was part of FTC’s services 

We then made iterations based on our findings from previous user testing and started incorporating brand colors and visuals into our prototypes.

Final design

With our final prototype, we were able to effectively emphasize the track club’s philosophy to accurately showcase what makes FTC special to a prospective audience, redesign the website to look more professional and representative of the club and its brand, as well as restructure the organization for users to be able to find information easily .
